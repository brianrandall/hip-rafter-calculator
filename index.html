The Math
Why It Works

Picture both roof slopes rising from the same point. The shallow roof rises 4" over 12", the steep roof rises 12" over 12". The vertical gap between them is 8". Your miter is the angle of that shallow rise against the gap -- a right triangle with legs 4 and 8. That's it.


The bevel is even simpler: it's just the pitch angle of the steeper roof. Tilt the blade to match that plane and the cut face aligns perfectly with it.

Specific to hip rafter cheek cut, plumb face down. Not the same as a jack rafter side cut.
